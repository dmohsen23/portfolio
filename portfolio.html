Dynamics of Trust in XAI: Perceived vs. Demonstrated Trust

Published research investigating the fundamental gap between perceived trust (self-reported) and demonstrated trust (actual AI delegation behavior) in XAI-assisted decision-making. Designed a human-subject experiment on Prolific with 360 participants, using a mushroom classification task with SHAP explanations, AdaBoost ML model, evaluative vs. non-evaluative AI modes, and high/low risk treatments. Applied MANOVA, ANOVA, ANCOVA, and Tukey HSD for statistical analysis.

About Me

Who I Am

I'm a data scientist and machine learning engineer with a strong foundation in building complete, end-to-end ML solutions. My work spans the full pipeline — from data collection and cleaning, through exploratory analysis and feature engineering, to model selection and final prediction.

I specialize in supervised learning for tabular and temporal data, with hands-on experience working with real-world messy datasets that require thoughtful preprocessing and domain-informed feature design.

My approach to ML is always motivation-first: every modeling choice — from the algorithm to the hyperparameter range — is driven by understanding the domain and the data, not just plugging in black-box tools.
